Optimal Racing Line Generator

Jupyter Notebook • Original LinkedIn post • GitHub Repository

Summer 2025

From my LinkedIn:
"The goal is to find a path that minimises both curvature and length, so less steering is required. Professionally, similar techniques are used to optimise both fuel efficiency and lap times in motorsport.

My solution uses B-spline smoothing to interpolate the initial track shape, and dynamic programming to iteratively produce the final optimal path. This approach avoids combinatorial explosion by efficiently selecting minimal-cost paths at each iteration, rather than brute-forcing every possibility. It required a fair amount of low-level manipulation of splines, as well as calculating geometric curvature and arc length via numerical integration.

Cost functions quantify how well a solution fits target criteria. I had to manually design, tune, and normalise my own polynomial cost function, balancing curvature and path length while adding an angle penalty to discourage sharp corners."
